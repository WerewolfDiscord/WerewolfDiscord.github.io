Randium 30-Nov-17 09:59
===========================
Latest news: New announcement!
===========================
The last two games, we have gained complaints about people being inactive for a long time. The game has been designed in such a manner that it is allowed to not be very active, but it felt dishonest to some active players that they died very quickly, and the inactives got to see the end of the game without doing anything.

We have decided not to discourage absence, but instead encourage presence. You can now earn blood points as a player, when you are really active.

How do you earn blood points?
Did you say something funny? Did you just mislead another player in a masterful way? Did you make a great discovery? Did you have a good talk? Were you roleplaying well? Then the @Game Masters will add the reaction to your message. This means that you have earned a blood point.

I've got a few blood points. So what?
When you have reached a certain amount of blood points (we will keep it a secret for now), you will gain a DM of the @Game Masters, which will tell you that you reached the maximum. With your blood points, you can buy an invisibility cloak that will prevent you from getting killed by any team. (All other effects, like curses, fortune telling, crowd seeking etc. will still work)

Can I stack my invisibility cloaks?
No. When you buy an invisibility cloak, you will lose all your earned blood points. Once you used your invisibility cloak, you can start earning blood points again.

Can I give my invisibility cloak away?
Sure! If you think you're safe, but your ally needs to be protected, you can just give your cloak away to someone in need. They will know they gained a free cloak, but they won't know who gave it to them. So you'll get to choose whether you tell them it was you...(edited)
